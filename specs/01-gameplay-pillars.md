# 01 — Gameplay Pillars

## 1. Readability over realism

The player should be able to understand direction, threat, and space at a glance.

Examples:
- obvious ground pattern repetition
- uncluttered silhouettes
- visible projectiles
- high-contrast HUD

Anti-examples:
- photoreal textures
- dense scenery
- muddy post-processing
- tiny unreadable projectiles

## 2. Fast response

Movement, looking, and firing should feel immediate.

Examples:
- low-latency input response
- short weapon cooldown
- crisp restart flow

Anti-examples:
- heavy inertia
- delayed fire feedback
- long transitions

## 3. Sparse world, strong landmarks

The world should be mostly open but memorable.

Examples:
- occasional tall pillars, cubes, arches, or spheres
- landmarks spaced far enough apart to act as orientation beacons

Anti-examples:
- cluttered obstacle field
- every area looking the same

## 4. Arcade shooting feel

Combat should be legible and satisfying.

Examples:
- visible projectiles
- hit flash
- simple damage states
- immediate score reward

Anti-examples:
- simulation-heavy recoil
- hidden damage confirmation
- complicated ammo systems in MVP

## 5. Retro audiovisual restraint

The project should suggest old-school style through simplicity, not novelty overload.

Examples:
- geometric meshes
- limited material complexity
- synthetic short sound effects

Anti-examples:
- excessive particle spam
- cinematic realism
- complex orchestral audio
